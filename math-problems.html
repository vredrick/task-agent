Advanced Mathematics Problem Set

Selected Problems in Analysis, Algebra, and Applied Mathematics

Problem 1 Graduate Level
Let $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ be a twice differentiable function. Prove that if $f$ satisfies the Poisson equation $$\Delta f = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i^2} = g(x)$$ where $g \in L^2(\mathbb{R}^n)$, then the fundamental solution can be expressed as: $$f(x) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} G(x-y) g(y) \, dy$$ Find the explicit form of the Green's function $G(x)$ for $n = 3$.
Category: Partial Differential Equations
Problem 2 Advanced
Consider the sequence of functions $\{f_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ defined on $[0,1]$ by: $$f_n(x) = \frac{nx}{1 + n^2x^2}$$
  1. Prove that $\{f_n\}$ converges pointwise to the zero function.
  2. Show that $\sup_{x \in [0,1]} |f_n(x)| = \frac{1}{2n}$.
  3. Determine whether the convergence is uniform on $[0,1]$.
  4. Calculate $\lim_{n \to \infty} \int_0^1 f_n(x) \, dx$ and compare with $\int_0^1 \lim_{n \to \infty} f_n(x) \, dx$.
Category: Real Analysis
Problem 3 Research Level
Let $A$ be a $C^*$-algebra and consider the GNS construction for a positive linear functional $\omega: A \to \mathbb{C}$. $$(\mathcal{H}_\omega, \pi_\omega, \Omega_\omega)$$ where $\mathcal{H}_\omega$ is the GNS Hilbert space, $\pi_\omega: A \to \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_\omega)$ is the GNS representation, and $\Omega_\omega$ is the cyclic vector. Prove that if $\omega$ is a pure state, then $\pi_\omega$ is an irreducible representation. Furthermore, show that the von Neumann algebra $\pi_\omega(A)''$ generated by $\pi_\omega(A)$ satisfies: $$\pi_\omega(A)'' = \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_\omega)$$
Category: Operator Algebras
Problem 4 Undergraduate
Consider the matrix differential equation: $$\frac{dX}{dt} = AX + XB$$ where $A, B \in \mathbb{M}_n(\mathbb{R})$ are constant matrices and $X(t) \in \mathbb{M}_n(\mathbb{R})$.
  1. Show that if $X(0) = X_0$, the solution can be written as: $$X(t) = e^{At} X_0 e^{Bt}$$
  2. Verify this solution by direct substitution.
  3. Find the solution when $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$, $B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, and $X_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
Category: Differential Equations
Problem 5 Graduate Level
Let $(X, \mathcal{F}, \mu)$ be a probability space and $\{T_t\}_{t \geq 0}$ be a strongly continuous semigroup of contractions on $L^2(X, \mu)$ with infinitesimal generator $A$. Define the resolvent operators $R_\lambda = (\lambda I - A)^{-1}$ for $\lambda > 0$.
  1. Prove the resolvent identity: $$R_\lambda - R_\mu = (\mu - \lambda) R_\lambda R_\mu$$
  2. Show that $\|R_\lambda\| \leq \frac{1}{\lambda}$ for all $\lambda > 0$.
  3. Establish the relationship: $$T_t f = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(I - \frac{t}{n}A\right)^{-n} f = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(\frac{n}{t}R_{n/t}\right)^n f$$
Category: Functional Analysis
Problem 6 Advanced
Consider the optimization problem: $$\min_{u \in H^1_0(\Omega)} J(u) = \frac{1}{2} \int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2 \, dx + \int_\Omega F(u) \, dx$$ where $\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ is a bounded domain with smooth boundary, $H^1_0(\Omega)$ is the Sobolev space, and $F: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ is a given function.
  1. Derive the Euler-Lagrange equation for this variational problem.
  2. Assume $F'(s) = s^3 - s$. Show that critical points satisfy: $$-\Delta u = u^3 - u \quad \text{in } \Omega, \quad u = 0 \quad \text{on } \partial\Omega$$
  3. Prove that if $u$ is a solution and $\lambda_1$ is the first eigenvalue of $-\Delta$ on $\Omega$, then $\|u\|_{L^2} \geq \sqrt{2(\lambda_1 - 1)}$ (assuming $\lambda_1 > 1$).
Category: Calculus of Variations
Problem 7 Research Level
Let $M$ be a compact Riemannian manifold of dimension $n$ with Ricci curvature $\text{Ric} \geq (n-1)K$ for some constant $K > 0$. Consider the eigenvalue problem for the Laplace-Beltrami operator: $$\Delta_g f + \lambda f = 0$$ Let $0 = \lambda_0 < \lambda_1 \leq \lambda_2 \leq \cdots$ be the eigenvalues.
  1. Prove that $\lambda_1 \geq nK$ (generalization of Lichnerowicz's theorem).
  2. Show that if equality holds, then $(M, g)$ is isometric to the round sphere $S^n(\frac{1}{\sqrt{K}})$.
  3. For the case $n = 2$, establish the refined bound: $$\lambda_1 \geq 2K + \frac{4\pi \chi(M)}{\text{Area}(M)}$$ where $\chi(M)$ is the Euler characteristic.
Category: Differential Geometry
Problem 8 Graduate Level
Let $\zeta(s)$ be the Riemann zeta function. Consider the Dirichlet eta function: $$\eta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^s} = (1 - 2^{1-s})\zeta(s)$$
  1. Prove that $\eta(s)$ converges for $\Re(s) > 0$ and provides an analytic continuation of $\zeta(s)$ to the half-plane $\Re(s) > 0$, $s \neq 1$.
  2. Show that the functional equation for $\zeta(s)$ implies: $$\eta(s) = 2^{s-1}\pi^{s-1}\sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2}\right)\Gamma(1-s)\eta(1-s)$$
  3. Use this to compute $\eta(-1), \eta(-3), \eta(-5)$ and relate them to Bernoulli numbers.
Category: Analytic Number Theory
